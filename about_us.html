About Swedish Ultraflyers (SUF) club

Swedish Ultraflyers (SUF) was formed in 1984 to create an ultra-light flying club in Upplands Väsby with Einar Edland and Gunnar Rydergren as the founders. Gunnar was chairman and Einar the technical manager. To finance bying a Tierraan aircraft from KG Knutsson, we needed 10 people who were willing to personally guarantee the loan of SEK 110,000, which was the cost at that time. We had to borrow premises from one of the members where we could assemble our Tierra, SE-YHG.

We were eager and made many mistakes due to the fact that we did not read the instructions carefully enough, which meant that we missed the seat belts which was one of the first steps to assemble. To assemble these, we had to disassemble the entire aircraft. We probably built the cart 3 times before everything was ready. We dragged Tierran down to the frozen lake Norrviken, mounted wings and the test pilot / salesman, Krister Larsson (now Krister Segerdahl) sat down behind the wheel (it is a steering wheel in Tierran) and did a successful test flight. First up by the club members was Einar, as he was the only one with a flight certificate.

It was a lot of flying for Einar, because everyone wanted a test drive. The question was where we would place the business when the ice was gone. Einar landed on the snow (we had skis) at Frölunda farm, Kungsängen and knocked on the door of Claes af Ugglas and asked if we could be at their airport. After some time of reflection, we had to rent two hangar places, an oil shed, and a small bucket as a clubhouse and farmer's tank. Gunnar fixed so we got a phone call. The following year we had two Tierra and luckily it was then that we rented two hangar places.After a few years, we moved into a real clubhouse that was sponsored by "Skåningarna" and we fixed electricity, we dug a well and sent in water samples that were approved, and arranged drains in the closed system. We rented a larger farm tank from Hjelmco Oil, which is our main supplier of aviation soup. In 2000, we built an extra hangar and laid cement slabs in front of the hangars.

In 1986 we were hit by a long flight ban when it turned out that the wings could not withstand the number of "G" promised. KG Knutsson had to develop new wings from Poland that withstood what they were supposed to. After a year of downtime and loyal members, we were able to fly our machines again, which changed its name to Akka. All in all, we flew more than 2000 hours with our Tierra / Akka. Another problem with the aircraft type was that they had Rotax 532 two-stroke engines, which caused a lot of trouble and caused many landings, but due to low stable speed no major injuries occurred. We reckoned that for every fly hour we had to screw in two. Later came the Rotax 582 and the situation improved somewhat. The Akka aircraft was actually a good aircraft, if one ignores the engine problems.

In total we had four different Akkor in our club.In 1991, Einar bought a Cuby II, SE-YLM with a Rotax 503 of 50 hp which he typed into Sweden. It was a big step from Akka to Cuby II when the Cuby looked like a "real plane" and the comments when you went around with the machine were that "that is not ultra light". Einar had the Swedish agency at Cuby and also offered it with Rotax 912, a 4-stroke engine of 80 hp! The club took on this concept and our first Cuby II was SE-YLO which has served us since 1992. After an accident in 1994, the machine was rebuilt into EuroCub Mk.1. In 2000, SE-YLO was sold to Rolf Lewander, who at the time of writing is chairman of Swedish Ultraflyers. The club still has access to "Ludvig Olle". In 1995, EuroCub number 2, SE-YSG, was bought, which tragically completely crashed in 1997 and one member died. The reason was that the pilot had acute medical problems while flying.

Flight School

With 10 partners and only one with a certificate in 1984, the need for a flight school was great. We went to Ingvar Arnbacke at KSAK to get a school permit and flight instructor and in 1985 the pilot legend Olle Åhnstand gave in and started training the co-owners. Gunnar Rydergren was first in the fire, but his son, Åke, finished first. In 1988, Einar, Gunnar and Åke had accumulated more than 200 UL flight hours each, and could thus be further trained as UL instructors. Sweden's first flight instructor course for UL took place in the spring of 1988 in Eslöv with Kurt Bengtsson at the helm. It was two hard weeks with instructors from Ljungbyhed and the boys got to fly with Cessna 172 and 152 with military precision! Now the training got really fast and until the time of writing in 2001 we have trained more than 200 people for UL certificates and SUF is today Sweden's largest UL flight school and UL club with an hourly rate of about 1000 hours per year.
